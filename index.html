Chicago

Chicago（Chicago）is located in the Midwestern Illinois, Lake Michigan, one of the five largest North America lakes in the east , one of the famous international financial centers and the third most populous city in the United States(after New York and Los Angeles)

The first settlers(1779)

In the 18th century, the Chicago area was the territory of the Potawatomi Indian tribe. In 1779, a black businessman from Haiti, Jean Baptiste Point DuSable, settled in Chicago. He married the Bertha Wattoms and opened the first warehouse in the area on Chicago's North Shore。

City establishment (1837)

In 1816, Berta Vatomi and other nearby Indian tribes signed a St. Louis agreement with the US government, giving up the land. The town of Chicago was founded on August 12, 1833, with 350 residents. With the increase in settling population, Chicago's population was only 4,179 in 1837

Chicago Fire (1871)

A fire broke out in Chicago from October 8 to 10, 1871. Almost all of the buildings in the urban area (including commercial areas) on about 6.5 square kilometers of land were burned out. About 300 people died, 90,000 were homeless, and property The losses amounted to $ 200 million. This is the famous Chicago Fire in American history.。

Strong development (19th century)

Chicago became a major city and agricultural distribution center in the Midwestern United States in the 19th century. In 1848, Chicago began to become an important transportation hub connecting the east and west of the United States. Convenient water and land transportation has greatly stimulated the development of industry and commerce. The prosperity of the city has attracted many outsiders to settle here. Between 1870 and 1900, Chicago's population soared from 299,000 to 1.7 million. The local manufacturing and retail industries have become the dominant force in the Midwest economy and have greatly affected the US economy..

Prosperity of the city (1950s)

Since the 1950s, middle- and upper-level residents gradually began to migrate to urban suburbs, resulting in the decline and destitution of many parts of the city center. During this period, Chicago hosted the 1968 Democratic National Convention and completed the iconic Hills Tower, McCormick Place and O'Hare Airport. The current mayor, Richard M. Daley, has a policy focused on improving the environmental quality of Chicago, implementing projects to revive declining neighborhoods, improve urban infrastructure, and urban landscapes along lakes and rivers. Chicago had bid for the 2016 Olympics, but was eliminated in the first round of voting on October 2, 2009。
